US banknotes

American dollars are the most popular foreign currency in
Russia. Briefly about the history of the dollar. Before talking
about the bill itself, it is worth finding out what the name
"dollar" means and how it appeared.

The name "dollar" is secondary to the name of
the European silver coin. Its appearance falls on
the period from 1775 to 1783 (during the
American War of Independence). Initially, dollars
were only in the form of coins, paper bills began
to be printed much later.
Until 1861, there was no such financial
structure as a Central Bank in the United States.
That is, for about 75 years, there has been no
control over the issue of dollars.

An interesting fact is the origin of the green color of the bills. The fact is that at the time of
printing the banknotes, it was discovered that there is more green paint in the warehouse of
the design company than other shades. As a result, this nuance has become a tradition.

The specifics of banknotes

— The process of manufacturing the product takes place in strict secrecy. This applies to the
composition of the paint, to the subtleties of security details, symbols, etc. This information is
top secret and inaccessible.

— Banknotes are made on sheets that do not include wood material. It consists of flax and
cotton fibers (in proportions of 25 * 75%). Information about the structure is also classified as
unavailable.

— Absolutely all banknotes, no matter what denomination they are, have the same
dimensions (15.6 * 6.63 cm).

— One banknote weighs about 1 gram. The weight may change in the direction of increase, as
it may be influenced by modern elements that are added to bills.

— The design of banknotes has changed. In addition to green shades, other colors have
appeared (orange, purple, blue and pink), which is done in order to increase protection.

— Each denomination has its own period of use. Money is actively used in the global space,
which contributes to rapid wear and tear. The most common banknotes are 1.5 and 10 USD.
Their use is designed for 16-20 months. The banknotes, in denominations of 2.20, 50 and 100
dollars, are designed for turnover for 5-7 years.

Denominations of banknotes

1 dollar is the smallest banknote at face value. Its
obverse depicts the first American President George
Washington, who was in power from 1789 to 1797. On
the left side of the portrait is the seal of the US Treasury
Department, which depicts scales representing justice,
13 stars (this is the number of the first states) and the
year of formation of the ministry — 1789.
The word "one" is written on the reverse side, and. on
both sides there is a seal that certifies the authenticity
of the documents. On one side of the seal there is a
head, on the second — a pyramid, in the upper part of
which there is an eye. The inscription "In God we trust"
is inscribed at the top, which translates as "we believe
in God". A little above the slogan there is the slogan
"The United States of America".

If you conduct an experiment and ask familiar
people traveling to the United States to bring $ 2 in a
bill, it is unlikely that the request will be fulfilled. The
thing is that the $ 2 bill, which is included in the
standard list of US banknotes throughout the history of
the existence of the state, is very rare.
Standardized banknotes with a face value of $ 2 were
put into circulation in 1862. The money depicted a
portrait of A. Hamilton, the first secretary of the US
Treasury. But after 7 years, the bill changed, because
instead of Hamilton, it began to depict Thomas
Jefferson — the "father" of the American constitution
and concurrently the third president of America. On
the reverse side of the banknote was a reproduction of
the painting "Declaration of Independence" by J.
Trumbull. The first circulation consisted of several
million banknotes.

The five-dollar bill of the United States ($5) is the
monetary unit of the United States. The modern $5
banknote features a portrait of Abraham Lincoln, the
16th President of the United States (1861-1865), on the
obverse and the Lincoln Memorial on the reverse. All
the $5 banknotes issued today are Federal Reserve
banknotes. A $5 bill is sometimes called a "fin." The
term has German/Yiddish roots and is distantly related
to the English "five", but today it is much less common
than in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
Nowadays it is also sometimes called the “five". As of
December 2018, the average service life of a $5
banknote in circulation is 4.7 years before it is replaced
due to wear and tear. Approximately 6% of all paper
money issued by the Bureau of Engraving and Printing
of the U.S. Treasury in 2009 were 5-dollar bills.

50 dollars. On the face of this banknote is a portrait of Willis Grant, who served as President of the United States from 1869 to 1877. The portrait of the 18th American president was placed on these bills in 1913. On the back of the bill is an image of the Capitol. A modern example of a 50-dollar bill has been in circulation since 2006. Among the total amount of money supply, the share of "fifty kopecks" is 6%. The validity period of the bill is about 55 months.

The 100 US dollar bill is the most popular in terms of
the number of banknotes issued in the world. Now
there are about 1.3 trillion dollars in the form of a
hundred dollars. Many sincerely believe that only US
presidents are depicted on American banknotes. But
there are exceptions in the general series, and one of
them is Benjamin Franklin. He is one of the "founding
fathers", since his signature is on three documents (the
American Declaration of Independence and the
Constitution, as well as the Versailles Peace Treaty
of 1783), which became the foundation of US statehood.
Without Franklin, the center of the North American
continent might now consist of several dozen different countries.

Rare banknotes

The 500-dollar banknote is actively used by the US Treasury and the Federal Reserve for the
purpose of mutual settlements. As a rule, there are no owners of such money among ordinary
citizens. On the front side of the bill there is a portrait of William McKinley (American president,
the 25th in a row). The reverse side contains the number 500, the inscriptions "United States of
America" and "Five hundred dollars".

1,000 is often used to make large interbank transfers. During the Great Depression, a large
number of such banknotes were issued in America, and to date, they have not been
withdrawn in full. There are still 160 thousand banknotes in circulation. On one side of the bill is
a portrait of Stephen Grover Cleveland, whose presidency years: 1885-1889 and 1893-1897. The
other side does not contain any drawings, only blurred patterns and inscriptions "The United
States of America" and "One thousand dollars".

The 5,000 banknote contains, as the main element, a portrait of James Madison on the front
side, and the inscription "The United States of America" and "Five thousand dollars" is placed
on the back. According to the special department of the Federal Reserve, three banknotes of
this denomination are in museums, 183 copies in private collections.

10,000 is the original face value. To date, there is a single sample produced in 1918. Several
samples from 1934 are in private collections. The obverse side of the banknote contains
an image of Salmon Portland Chase, and the reverse side contains the inscriptions "The United
States of America" and the denomination "Ten thousand dollars".

100,000 banknote. On its front side there is a portrait of the 28th American President Thomas
Woodrow Wilson. On the reverse, in the central part — the number "100,000" and the
inscriptions "The United States of America" and "One hundred thousand dollars". Gray and
orange shades are used in the color scheme. A total of 42,000 samples were printed. 100-
-thousand banknotes have never been in circulation and were used exclusively for mutual
settlements between financial structures. Collectors have only a few copies at their disposal.

1,000,000 is a unique sample. The idea of making this banknote belongs to millionaire Tedi
Steward, the owner of a closed business club. This money token became a pass for the
participants of the meeting of this society. Despite the fact that the denomination is indicated
on the banknotes, in fact, they do not represent value. At auction, such a sign can be bought
for $ 100, sometimes the price rose to 9.5 thousand.
